Travel to Mumbai

1. Gateway of India

The grand arch on the waterfront is a symbol of the city and the starting point for seaside walks. It's a pleasant place to simply sit by the water, watch the moving city traffic and the colorful crowds, and take a photo with a view of the legendary Taj Mahal Palace hotel.

2. Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Railway Station

The historic Victorian neo-Gothic railway station is one of Mumbai's most beautiful buildings. Its fa√ßade, with its towers and stucco, is reminiscent of European cathedrals, while inside, you can experience the true Indian urban rhythm: trains, crowds, and street food right next to the station.

3. Marine Drive

A long, curved promenade along the Arabian Sea, nicknamed "The Queen's Necklace" in the evening because of its lights. It's best to stroll here at sunset: on one side is the sea and a fresh breeze, on the other, a line of high-rise buildings and the feeling of a vast, vibrant metropolis.
